Data Privacy Act (Republic Act 10173)

Overview

Republic Act No. 10173, also known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012, is the comprehensive data privacy law of the Philippines. It aims to protect the fundamental human right of privacy while ensuring the free flow of information for innovation, growth, and national development.

Who Must Comply

  • Personal Information Controllers (PICs) - Organizations that determine the purposes and means of processing personal data
  • Personal Information Processors (PIPs) - Organizations that process personal data on behalf of PICs
  • All businesses that collect, store, or process personal information of Filipino citizens
  • Government agencies handling personal data

Key Requirements

  • Registration: Register with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) if processing sensitive personal information or if you're a PIC with at least 1,000 individuals' data
  • Privacy Notice: Inform data subjects about data collection and processing purposes
  • Consent: Obtain proper consent before processing personal information
  • Data Protection Officer (DPO): Appoint a DPO if required by your business size and data processing activities
  • Security Measures: Implement organizational, physical, and technical security measures
  • Breach Notification: Report data breaches to NPC within 72 hours

Sensitive Personal Information

Special protection is required for:

  • Health, medical, and genetic information
  • Financial information and credit card details
  • Government-issued ID numbers (SSS, TIN, passport, driver's license)
  • Biometric data
  • Sexual orientation and marital status
  • Political affiliations and religious beliefs

Penalties

  • Unauthorized processing: ₱500,000 to ₱2,000,000 fine + 1-3 years imprisonment
  • Accessing due to negligence: ₱500,000 to ₱1,000,000 fine + 1-2 years imprisonment
  • Improper disposal: ₱100,000 to ₱500,000 fine + 6 months to 2 years imprisonment
  • Non-compliance with NPC orders: ₱100,000 to ₱1,000,000 fine

Getting Started

  1. Data Mapping: Identify what personal data you collect and how it's processed
  2. Legal Basis: Determine your legal basis for processing (consent, contract, legal obligation, etc.)
  3. Privacy Notice: Create and publish your privacy notice
  4. Security Measures: Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures
  5. Registration: Register with NPC if required
  6. Training: Train your staff on data privacy requirements

Useful Resources

  • National Privacy Commission: privacy.gov.ph
  • NPC Helpdesk: info@privacy.gov.ph
  • DPA Registration: Available through NPC online portal

Revised Corporation Code (Republic Act 11232)

Overview

Republic Act No. 11232, the Revised Corporation Code of the Philippines, modernizes corporate law and governance. It replaced the old Corporation Code (Batas Pambansa 68) and introduces significant reforms for business incorporation and operation.

Who Must Comply

  • All corporations organized under Philippine law
  • Foreign corporations doing business in the Philippines
  • Directors and officers of corporations
  • Stockholders and other stakeholders

Key Changes & Requirements

  • Perpetual Corporate Term: Corporations now have perpetual existence unless otherwise specified
  • One Person Corporation (OPC): Single-person corporations are now allowed
  • Reduced Capital Requirements: Minimum paid-up capital reduced to ₱25,000
  • Electronic Meetings: Stockholders' and board meetings can be held virtually
  • Electronic Voting: Remote voting is permitted for meetings
  • Corporate Recovery: Enhanced rehabilitation procedures for distressed corporations

Compliance Requirements

  • SEC Registration: File Articles of Incorporation and By-laws with SEC
  • Annual Reports: Submit General Information Sheet (GIS) and Financial Statements annually
  • Board Meetings: Hold regular board meetings and maintain minutes
  • Stockholders' Meetings: Conduct annual stockholders' meetings
  • Corporate Books: Maintain required corporate records and books
  • Tax Compliance: File corporate income tax returns and other tax obligations

Corporate Governance

  • Board Composition: Minimum of 5 directors, maximum of 15
  • Independent Directors: Required for publicly-listed companies
  • Corporate Secretary: Must be a Filipino citizen and resident
  • Treasurer: May be a non-Filipino but must be a resident
  • Audit Committee: Required for certain corporations

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • Failure to file reports: ₱1,000 per day of delay + possible delinquency
  • Operating without license: Fine of ₱1,000 to ₱10,000 per day
  • False statements: Criminal penalties under the Revised Penal Code
  • Corporate dissolution: For continued violations or ultra vires acts

Getting Started

  1. Name Reservation: Reserve your corporate name with SEC
  2. Articles of Incorporation: Prepare and notarize incorporation documents
  3. SEC Registration: Submit documents and pay registration fees
  4. BIR Registration: Obtain TIN and register for taxes
  5. Mayor's Permit: Apply for business permit from local government
  6. SSS/PhilHealth/Pag-IBIG: Register for social security benefits

Useful Resources

  • SEC Website: sec.gov.ph
  • SEC Hotline: (02) 8818-0921 to 24
  • Online Registration: SEC i-Register system

National Internal Revenue Code (Tax Code)

Overview

The National Internal Revenue Code (NIRC) governs taxation in the Philippines. It covers income tax, value-added tax (VAT), excise tax, documentary stamp tax, and other national internal revenue taxes administered by the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR).

Who Must Comply

  • All businesses operating in the Philippines
  • Corporations (domestic and foreign)
  • Partnerships and other business entities
  • Self-employed individuals and professionals
  • Employers with employees

Major Tax Types for Businesses

  • Corporate Income Tax: 25% of net taxable income (20% for domestic corporations with net taxable income not exceeding ₱5M and total assets not exceeding ₱100M)
  • Value-Added Tax (VAT): 12% on sale of goods and services
  • Withholding Taxes: Various rates depending on type of payment
  • Documentary Stamp Tax: On documents, instruments, and transactions
  • Fringe Benefits Tax: 35% on fringe benefits given to employees

Key Compliance Requirements

  • BIR Registration: Obtain Tax Identification Number (TIN) and register your business
  • Books of Accounts: Maintain proper accounting records
  • Tax Returns: File monthly, quarterly, and annual tax returns
  • Invoicing: Issue BIR-registered receipts and invoices
  • Withholding: Withhold and remit taxes from payments to suppliers/employees
  • Tax Payments: Pay taxes on time to avoid penalties

Filing Deadlines

  • Monthly VAT Returns: 20th day of the following month
  • Quarterly Income Tax: Last day of the month following each quarter
  • Annual Income Tax Return: April 15 of the following year
  • Withholding Tax Returns: 10th day of the following month
  • BIR Annual Registration Fee: January 31 of each year

Tax Incentives Available

  • CREATE Act Benefits: Reduced corporate income tax rates
  • PEZA Registration: Tax holidays and special rates for export businesses
  • BOI Incentives: For preferred investment activities
  • Barangay Micro Business Enterprises (BMBEs): Tax exemptions for qualifying small businesses
  • OSD (Optional Standard Deduction): 40% of gross income instead of itemized deductions

Penalties for Non-Compliance

  • Late filing: 25% of tax due or ₱1,000, whichever is higher
  • Late payment: 20% of tax due plus 12% annual interest
  • Failure to file: 50% of tax due or ₱10,000, whichever is higher
  • Tax evasion: Fine of ₱500,000 to ₱10,000,000 plus imprisonment
  • Non-registration: Closure of business and criminal penalties

Getting Started

  1. BIR Registration: Apply for TIN and register your business type
  2. Authority to Print: Get authorization to print receipts/invoices
  3. Accounting System: Set up proper bookkeeping and accounting
  4. Tax Calendar: Create a calendar of filing and payment deadlines
  5. Professional Help: Consider hiring a CPA or tax consultant

Useful Resources

  • BIR Website: bir.gov.ph
  • BIR Hotline: (02) 8981-8888
  • eFPS (Electronic Filing and Payment System): Online tax filing platform
  • Revenue Regulations: Available on BIR website

Labor Code of the Philippines

Overview

The Labor Code of the Philippines (Presidential Decree 442) governs employment relationships, worker rights, and employer obligations. It covers hiring, wages, working conditions, termination, and social benefits for employees.

Who Must Comply

  • All employers with employees in the Philippines
  • Corporations, partnerships, and sole proprietorships
  • Foreign companies with Filipino employees
  • Government contractors and service providers
  • Manpower agencies and recruitment firms

Key Employment Requirements

  • Employment Contracts: Written contracts for all employees
  • Minimum Wage: Comply with regional wage orders
  • Work Hours: Maximum 8 hours per day, 48 hours per week
  • Overtime Pay: 25% premium for work beyond 8 hours
  • Holiday Pay: Regular and special holiday compensation
  • 13th Month Pay: Mandatory year-end bonus equivalent to 1/12 of annual salary

Mandatory Benefits

  • SSS (Social Security System): Social security coverage
  • PhilHealth: Health insurance coverage
  • Pag-IBIG: Housing development fund contribution
  • Service Incentive Leave: 5 days paid leave after 1 year of service
  • Maternity/Paternity Leave: 105 days for mothers, 7 days for fathers
  • Separation Pay: For authorized causes of termination

Working Conditions & Safety

  • Occupational Safety and Health: Provide safe working environment
  • Workers' Compensation: Coverage for work-related injuries
  • Night Shift Differential: 10% premium for work between 10 PM to 6 AM
  • Rest Periods: Minimum 24-hour rest period per week
  • Meal Breaks: At least 60 minutes for meals during shifts exceeding 6 hours

Termination and Due Process

  • Just Causes: Serious misconduct, willful disobedience, gross negligence
  • Authorized Causes: Redundancy, retrenchment, business closure
  • Due Process: Written notice and opportunity to be heard
  • Security of Tenure: Employees cannot be dismissed without just cause
  • Final Pay: Must include all earned wages and benefits

Labor Standards Compliance

  • DOLE Registration: Register establishment with Department of Labor
  • Work Rules: Post company policies and labor standards
  • Payroll Records: Maintain detailed employee records
  • Labor Standards Posting: Display employee rights and company policies
  • Inspection Compliance: Allow DOLE inspections and comply with findings

Penalties for Violations

  • Underpayment of wages: Payment of deficiency plus 10% annual interest
  • Illegal dismissal: Reinstatement plus full back wages
  • Labor standards violations: Fines of ₱25,000 to ₱100,000
  • Safety violations: Fines up to ₱100,000 plus possible closure
  • Repeat violations: Higher penalties and possible criminal charges

Getting Started

  1. DOLE Registration: Register your establishment with local DOLE office
  2. Employee Handbook: Create comprehensive work rules and policies
  3. Payroll System: Implement proper payroll and timekeeping systems
  4. Benefits Registration: Register with SSS, PhilHealth, and Pag-IBIG
  5. Safety Program: Develop occupational safety and health program
  6. Legal Compliance: Regular review of labor law updates

Useful Resources

  • DOLE Website: dole.gov.ph
  • DOLE Hotline: 1349 (for labor complaints)
  • National Wages and Productivity Commission: For wage order updates
  • BWC (Bureau of Working Conditions): For working conditions guidance
